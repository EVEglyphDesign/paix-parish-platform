The Charitable Ledger

The parish sites above are the visible part. The Charitable Ledger is what makes them worth something. A parish-scale mutual-aid economy that turns everyday shopping, an hour of service, and a professional's time into a common fund the parish itself controls — not Amazon, not Facebook, not GoDaddy.

In plain words, for a Brother Knight who works nights:

  • You already shop. Do it through the parish code and the difference between retail and wholesale lands in the parish fund, not in a warehouse in Seattle.
  • You already give an hour. When a Brother Knight witnesses it, that hour becomes one token in the same fund.
  • You already know a plumber, an accountant, a nurse. They can donate one hour at their real market rate. The parish gets the value; the professional keeps up to 30% as walk-away if they want it.
  • Every dollar and every hour is visible. One public dashboard, nine numbers, refreshed nightly. No black box.

Alphonse Desjardins did this once with Catholic savings. Father McGivney did it once with Catholic insurance. This is the third turn of the same wheel.

What this is

Five Catholic parishes, each with a mirrored site under a shared editorial template. Same header, same footer, same navigation — but each parish keeps its own crest, mass times, contact information and diocesan link. If a parish already has an IT volunteer, this is not a replacement; it is an option they can inspect, fork, or ignore.

Locally hosted

Static site on GitHub Pages or the parish's own hosting. No third-party dashboards, no vendor lock-in.

Zero surveillance

No cookies. No analytics. No third-party scripts other than Google Fonts. noindex, nofollow until the parish approves publication.

Full parish ownership

The parish owns its content, its domain, and its exit. Fork the repository, take it home, keep going.

Consistent design

Shared EVE Glyph Design canon so parishioners moving between parish sites feel at home.

For the Knights of Columbus

This is what "scale in a week" looks like — with no hurt feelings.

If the Knights want to standardize parish web presence across a diocese, this template can spin up a new parish site in about an hour. But that is not the offer. The offer is a choice:

  • Existing IT volunteer stays put. If a Brother Knight or parishioner is already running the parish site, nothing is disrupted. This platform is available if they want to switch or fork; otherwise it sits alongside their existing work as a reference.
  • Money stays in the parish. No annual GoDaddy invoices, no Squarespace subscriptions, no Facebook advertising credits. Static hosting on GitHub Pages is free, and every dollar the parish saves stays in the community.
  • Parishioners stay safe. No surveillance, no engagement-optimized feeds, no algorithmic exposure of children, women, or vulnerable people. The parish site is a parish site — not a data-extraction surface for a platform in California.
  • The Knights stay in charge. Ownership, doctrine and exit rights belong to the parish and its Council. The design canon is a shared reference, not a vendor contract.

Council of Palms #6673 (Holy Trinity, Lenexa) is the reference council for this build.

The economic engine that pairs with the parish sites is the Charitable Ledger above. That is the offer the Knights are being invited to weigh, not just the websites.
